# ¡Hola, soy Ismael!

I’m from both California and Baja California and I am a second year master's student at [USC's Viterbi School of Engineering](https://viterbischool.usc.edu/) studying computer science. I got my B.S. at [Columbia University's Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science](https://www.engineering.columbia.edu/) (that's quite the mouthful) for computer science as well.

I am primarily interested in computer science through the context of diversity and inclusion. What does that mean? Doesn't that encompass so much? Well, yea! I am very interested in the many ways that computer science interacts with diversity problems not only in the systems that we create, but also in terms of the industry with inclusion. But if you're still a bit unsure as to what I mean, here's a quick list of ideas that I'd like to study:
* Representation in computer systems
	* Virtual Humans
		* Presentation with different ethnicities
		* Speak how we speak!
			* Informal speaking
			* Spanish-English code-switching (aka Spanglish)
			* Slang
	* Ethical ML
		* Identifying bias in ML systems and fixing them
* CS Education for underrepresented communities

Also, I have been interested in various aspects of computer science research. At Columbia, I worked on computational linguistics with something near and dear to my heart – Spanish-English codeswitching. I enjoy seeing the intersection of everyday natural communication with machines and how they understand us. The summer of 2018, I was at UC Berkeley working on cognition and perception of drivers in smart vehicles. I guess there is a pattern here where I like to see how machines can help make human lives more convenient and exciting.
